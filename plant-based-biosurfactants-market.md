<p><p>The plant-based biosurfactants market comprises three main types: sophorolipids, rhamnolipids, and others. Sophorolipids are glycolipids derived from yeast, known for their effectiveness in reducing surface tension and enhancing emulsification. Rhamnolipids, produced by certain bacteria, are valued for their biodegradability and role in environmental remediation. Other biosurfactants include glucose-based surfactants and fatty acid derivatives, which offer diverse applications across industries such as cosmetics, agriculture, and cleaning products, emphasizing the shift towards sustainable and eco-friendly solutions.</p></p>
